
# Lab 04 - User Persona

> Replace the practice example with your own group case.

## 1. Persona Name

Aung Aung, Second-Year ICT Student

---

## 2. User Type

University student who has difficulty finding study partners and study groups for the same subject.

---

## 3. Background and Context

- Aung Aung studies Computer Science at university.
- He usually experiences this problem before quizzes, assignments, and final exams when he needs classmates to study with.
- He currently uses Facebook Groups, Messenger, LINE, Google Classroom, and class group chats to look for study partners.

---

## 4. Goals

- Find study partners quickly.
- Join study groups for the same subject.
- Prepare for exams with classmates.
- Save time instead of searching through many social media groups.

---

## 5. Pain Points from Lab 03 Evidence

| Pain Point | Evidence ID / Respondent | Explanation |
|------------|--------------------------|-------------|
| Difficult to find study partners | R1, R3, R5, R7, R8 | Most respondents said it is difficult to find classmates studying the same subject. |
| Responses in Facebook or Messenger groups are slow | R2, R4, R6 | Students often wait a long time for replies or receive no response. |
| Class group chats are unorganized | R9, R10 | Important messages are buried among many unrelated messages. |

---

## 6. Current Alternatives / Workarounds

| Current Alternative | Weakness / Limitation | Evidence |
|---------------------|-----------------------|----------|
| Facebook Groups | Too many unrelated posts and slow responses | Interview |
| Messenger Groups | Messages disappear quickly and are difficult to search | Interview |
| Class Group Chat | Not organized by subject | Interview |
| Asking Friends | Limited to personal network | Interview |

---

## 7. Design Implications

### What should the system/app/platform support?

- Search study groups by subject.
- Create new study groups.
- Join existing study groups.
- Display study group information clearly.
- Filter groups by subject or course.

### What should the team avoid?

- Complicated registration process.
- Too many unnecessary features in the MVP.
- Confusing navigation.
- Unorganized group listings.

### Which user need is most important for the MVP?

The most important need is allowing students to quickly search for and join study groups based on their subjects.
